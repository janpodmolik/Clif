# Person A vs Person B

**Hook template:** Person A vs Person B (kontrast / humor / positioning)
**Format:** Split-screen. You play both sides. Left = Person A, right = Person B. **Voiceover (you, off-camera narrator) carries the whole script.** Person A and Person B don't speak — they react silently. The only spoken line from the split is L's "…me?" in chunk 3.
**Length:** 20–30s
**Goal:** Position Uuumi against Apple Screen Time by making the "Ignore Limit" button feel ridiculous next to a pet you can lose.

---

## Variant B — Confrontational ("you have no friend")

| Chunk | Voiceover (VO) | Visual on split |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | "Person A uses Apple Screen Time." | L holds iPhone, neutral. R still, expressionless. |
| 2 | "Person B has a friend that dies if they scroll too much." | R holds iPhone, dead serious. L slowly turns head toward R, confused. |
| 3 | *(VO silent — only spoken line in the split)* L: **"…me?"** | L looks at R, small voice. R doesn't move. |
| 4 | "No. You're using Apple Screen Time. You have no friend." | R slowly turns to L, deadpan. L looks down at phone. |
| 5 | *(VO silent — let the moment breathe ~1s)* | L processes. Tiny shoulder slump. No defense. |
| 6 | "Person B has Uuumi." | R looks back at camera, calm. L still defeated on the side. |
| 7 | *(VO silent — text overlay: "Uuumi — link in bio")* | Both look at camera. Hold ~2s. |

**Delivery notes:**
- VO is **deadpan throughout**. Narrator energy, not enthusiastic announcer. No smile in voice.
- "You have no friend" only works if VO sounds like it's stating a fact, not a roast. The comedy is in the absurd logical leap (using Apple Screen Time ≠ friendlessness), not in cruelty.
- L's "…me?" must be **small, genuinely confused**, not comedic. The contrast between L's small spoken line and the heavy VO is the joke.
- The silent beat in chunk 5 is critical — that's where the punchline lands. Don't fill it with VO.

## Variant C — Aha moment ("a pet on a floating island")

| Chunk | Voiceover (VO) | Visual on split |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | "Person A uses Apple Screen Time." | L holds iPhone, neutral. R still. |
| 2 | "Person B has a friend that dies if they scroll too much." | R holds iPhone, dead serious. L slowly turns head toward R, confused. |
| 3 | *(VO silent — only spoken line in the split)* L: **"…me?"** | L looks at R, head tilt, curious. R doesn't move. |
| 4 | "No. A pet on a floating island." | R turns to L, calm. L's expression softens — still confused, not threatened. |
| 5 | *(VO silent ~1s — let the question hang)* | L glances at R's phone, then back at R. Looks like she wants to ask "that's a friend?" but doesn't. |
| 6 | "When Person A hits their limit, they just tap an ignore button. When Person B hits their limit, the only way to keep scrolling is to blow their friend off the island. Forever. Lose all the progress." | Mid-line: brief B-roll cut to Apple "Ignore Limit" tap (1s) → cut back to split. Later mid-line: B-roll of Uuumi pet being blown away (1s) → back to split. |
| 7 | *(VO silent — text overlay: "Uuumi — link in bio")* | Both look at camera. Hold ~2s. |

**Delivery notes:**
- L is curious, not pathetic. Head tilt, not slump.
- Chunk 6 is the longest VO line — this is where the **mechanic + stake** is fully delivered. Pacing: split it into two halves with a tiny pause at "…Forever." That pause is the punchline.
- B-roll inserts in chunk 6 are optional but powerful — they make the abstract stake (blow away) visually concrete. Keep each insert ≤1s, otherwise the split-screen rhythm breaks.
- VO tone: matter-of-fact, narrator. Don't sell the app — describe the scenario.

## Shared visual & pacing

- **Split:** clean vertical 50/50. Same / similar neutral background on both sides.
- **Wardrobe:** one small differentiator (hoodie up/down, glasses on/off, t-shirt color). No costumes.
- **Cross-glance choreography:** L's head turn toward R in chunk 2 is the first comedy beat. R's slow turn to L in chunk 4 is the second. Practice these — timing makes or breaks the joke.
- **Pacing target:** 7 chunks in ~22–25s.
  - Variant B: tighter, ~22s (silent beats are short).
  - Variant C: longer, ~28s (chunk 6 is the long VO + B-roll inserts).
- **Audio:**
  - Clean VO, recorded separately, mixed over the split-screen footage.
  - No music under speech. Optional: single subtle wind-gust SFX on the word "dies" (chunk 2) and "Forever" (variant C, chunk 6).
- **L's "me?"** is the only on-camera audio. Record it clean, sync precisely. Make sure VO doesn't overlap.

## Description

**Variant B:** "Apple Screen Time vs Uuumi. Person A vs Person B. Link in bio."
**Variant C:** "Person A has Apple Screen Time. Person B has a friend that dies if they scroll. Uuumi, link in bio."

## Risks to watch

- **L is the viewer.** Don't humiliate L — L is curious or quietly defeated, never stupid. The viewer needs to identify with L to want to switch to B.
- **VO must be deadpan.** If you sound enthusiastic ("hey guys, today…"), the format collapses. Narrator voice, not creator voice.
- **Don't break the split for too long.** B-roll inserts ≤1s. The split-screen IS the format — fight to stay in it.
- **L's "me?" is the entire on-camera vocal performance.** It has to be perfect — small, confused, vulnerable. Re-record until it lands.

## Variations to try later

- **Reversed open:** start with chunk 2 (R's setup) before chunk 1 (Person A). Different curiosity gap.
- **Label cards:** "PERSON A" / "PERSON B" appear above each side for first 1–2s, then fade. Helps cold viewers orient.
- **15s ultra-short (variant B only):** drop chunk 5's silent beat, cut from "…me?" → "You have no friend." → CTA. Less landing time, more views.
- **No "me?" version:** make Person A 100% silent reaction. Replace chunk 3 with a long beat where L just stares at R. Risk: removes the only relatable human moment.
